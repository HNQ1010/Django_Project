AMERICAN ENGLISH - SPECIAL CASE

    1. ON

    • Preposition:
      • On a surface of something. 
                      I put the book on that shelf.
                He stepped on my foot.
      Covering, touching, attached to, or hanging from something. 
                      You have blood on your shirt.
                Which finger do you wear your ring on?
      Use to show the date or day when something happens. 
                      He will arrive on February 14.
                I have to work on Saturday.
      Used to refer to a place when giving directions. 
                      Make the first turn on the right.
                Our house is the second on the left after the stop sign.
      In a particular place. 
                      See the diagram on page 22.
                I met her on a ship.
